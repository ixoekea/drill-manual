GLOSSARY
Advance To turn, or move, toward the front.
Cadence The number of paces per minute.
Colour A flag or banner used to identify a nation, group, or organisation.
Cover An act by which an individual in a formation places himself directly behind another within a file.
Dressing The vertical and horizontal alignment of the ranks and files of a formation.
File A subgroup of personnel formed up and aligned one behind another as part of a larger formation such as a squad or platoon.
File, blank A spot in a file that is unoccupied by personnel.
File, single A formation composed of one line of personnel arranged one behind another.
Flank The side of a formation. A formation has only two flanks: the right flank and the left flank.
Flank, directing The flank by which a formation takes its dressing. The directing flank is normally the side on which the marker is located.
Flank, inner The directing flank serving as a pivot when a formation changes direction.
Flank, outer The flank opposite the inner flank.
Form A movement by which a formation in line changes direction 90 degrees without changing shape.
Form squad A movement by which a formation in file changes shape to that of being in line without changing direction.
Formation A generic term for an organised body of personnel; e.g., squad, platoon. A formation can contain one, two, or three ranks.
Front The direction a formation faces. The front remains the same from the time a formation falls in until the formation is dismissed.
Half pace A pace of 35 cm. (approximately 14 inches) as measured from the heel of the rear foot to the heel of the front foot.
Incline To change direction by 45 degrees.
Interval The distance between adjacent ranks or adjacent files.
Left The direction to the left of a formation. The left remains the same from the time a formation falls in until the formation is dismissed.
Marker A designated individual, usually the number one person of the front rank, from whom all other personnel in the formation take their dressing.
Mark time To march in one spot.
Move to the left To turn, or move, toward the left.
Move to the right To turn, or move, toward the right.
Pace A standard stride of 75 cm. (approximately 30 inches) as measured from the heel of the rear foot to the heel of the front foot.
Parade (verb) To formally assemble on a parade ground for drill and ceremonial.
Parade (noun) The sum of all units assembled on a parade ground for drill and ceremonial.
Pay compliments Render a salute to entitled persons or parties.
Pike The pole on which Colours or other flags are mounted for carrying or display.
Platoon A body of personnel formed up on parade.
Rank A group of personnel formed up and aligned in line abreast, usually as part of a larger formation such as a squad or platoon.
Rear The direction behind a formation. The rear remains the same from the time a formation falls in until the formation is dismissed.
Retire To turn, or move, toward the rear.
Right The direction to the right of a formation. The right remains the same from the time a formation falls in until the formation is dismissed.
Squad A body of personnel formed up for the purpose of receiving drill instruction.
Stand fast To remain in position; to not move.
Standard pace A pace of 75 cm. (approximately 30 inches) as measured from the heel of the rear foot to the heel of the front foot.
Turn To change direction by 90 degrees. A turn executed by a formation will change both the direction and shape of the formation.
Wheel A movement by which a formation, formed up either in file or in line, changes direction by up to 90 degrees without changing shape.
